## Executive Summary

Built two new concepts for Josh's review, both focused on **strategic signals vs operational metrics**:

1. **VP Strategic Pulse** (Weekly) — Comprehensive allocation market intelligence
2. **VP Daily Brief** (Daily) — 1-page action-focused snapshot

Both versions shift from **"what happened yesterday"** (tactical) to **"what do I need to know to lead through market transition"** (strategic).

## Why Different from Existing Versions?

### Current Versions (Built May-June)

| Version | Focus | User | Issue for VP Level |
|---------|-------|------|-------------------|
| **Comprehensive** | 13 operational metrics (RFQ lines, CQ conversion, response times) | Regional Managers | Too tactical — VP doesn't manage quote velocity |
| **Action-First** | Same metrics, reordered with Executive Brief | Regional Managers | Still operational — priority sorting doesn't change that VP doesn't act on "MEX 2% CQ conversion" |

**Core problem:** Both existing versions answer "what's flowing through the system today?" — useful for managers executing, not for VP making strategic calls.

### New VP Versions (Built Today)

| Version | Focus | Cadence | Strategic Value |
|---------|-------|---------|-----------------|
| **VP Strategic Pulse** | Allocation market signals, line card strategy, regional intelligence, pipeline risk | Weekly | Positions Josh to lead through market transition (constrained → allocated) |
| **VP Daily Brief** | Top 3 strategic actions, market signals, regional status, allocation-exposed deals | Daily | 1-page "what matters today" — scans in 60 seconds |

**Core shift:** Answer "what market forces are emerging and how prepared are we?" — VP decision-making level.

## Market Context (Critical for Meeting)

### Allocation Market Cycle

```
Normal → Constrained → Allocated → Recovery
         (we are here)
```

**Stages:**
1. **Normal:** Supply meets demand, standard lead times
2. **Constrained:** Demand exceeds supply, lead times extend +50%
3. **Allocated:** Manufacturers ration supply, spot premiums rise
4. **Recovery:** Capacity catches up, lead times normalize

**Regional Pattern:** APAC enters phases 3-4 weeks before USA, 6-8 weeks before EMEA

**Current Signals:**
- APAC RFQ volume +87% (memory-heavy)
- Memory lead times: 10wks → 14.5wks avg (+45%)
- USA +12% (early warning, lags APAC by 3-4 weeks)
- MEX flat (not seeing trend yet)

**Strategic Response:** Added 14 tier 2/3 manufacturers (Apr-May) to support alternate recommendations when tier 1s allocate

**Problem:** Only 3% of quotes use tier 2/3 alternates — readiness gap before allocation hits

## How They Work Together

### Recommended Cadence

| Report | Frequency | Purpose | Time to Read |
|--------|-----------|---------|--------------|
| **VP Daily Brief** | Mon-Fri, 6:00 AM EST | Quick health check + top 3 actions | 60 seconds |
| **VP Strategic Pulse** | Fridays, 6:00 AM EST | Deep market intelligence + line card strategy | 5-10 minutes |

### Information Architecture

**Daily Brief:**
- Answers: "What's changing RIGHT NOW and where do I focus today?"
- Alerts on deviations from strategy
- Flags emerging patterns

**Weekly Strategic Pulse:**
- Answers: "How are we positioned for market transition and what gaps exist?"
- Full allocation market intelligence
- Line card effectiveness analysis
- Regional leading indicators
- Pipeline risk assessment

---

## Key Differences from Manager-Level Reports

| Dimension | Manager Reports | VP Strategic Reports |
|-----------|----------------|---------------------|
| **Metrics** | RFQ lines entered, CQ conversion %, sourcing velocity | Market cycle signals, manufacturer concentration, regional divergence |
| **Timeframe** | Yesterday vs 5-day avg | 30-day trends, APAC leading USA by 3-4 weeks |
| **Actions** | "MEX has 2% CQ conversion — escalate to sourcing" | "APAC entering constrained phase — prep USA team for Q3 allocation" |
| **Granularity** | Individual sellers, specific quotes | Regional patterns, manufacturer exposure, line card strategy |
| **Purpose** | Execute daily operations | Position company for market transition |
