## 🎯 Top 3 Strategic Insights from Real Data

### 1. **B to I Ratio Collapse is THE Red Flag**

**What it means:**
- Q1: Booking $2.38 for every $1 invoiced (building backlog)
- Q2: Invoicing $1 for every $0.59 booked (draining backlog)

**Two scenarios:**
- ✅ **Good:** Clearing massive Q1 backlog, bookings normalize in Q3
- 🚨 **Bad:** Booking pipeline drying up (market slowdown or lost deals)

**Action for Josh:**
> "I need to see Q3 booking forecast NOW. If bookings don't recover, we're invoicing ourselves into a Q4 revenue cliff."

---

### 2. **June Delivery Concentration = Allocation Pattern**

**What it means:**
- $9.4M scheduled for June (80% of all backlog)
- July-December combined: $2.3M (20%)
- Normal distribution would be ~$2M/month

**Why this matters:**
- Suppliers forcing delivery windows (not continuous flow) = allocation behavior
- If June deliveries slip, H2 revenue projections collapse
- APAC pattern (366% performance) suggests suppliers rationing to specific delivery dates

**Action for Josh:**
> "Which suppliers make up that $9.4M? Are they the same ones we flagged for allocation risk?"

---

### 3. **Regional Divergence = Market Cycle Proof**

**What it means:**
- APAC: 366% to goal, 42% GM, $8.64M backlog
- USA: 83% to goal, no GM breakdown, $2.04M backlog
- MEX: 60% to goal, no GM breakdown, $554K backlog

**Why this validates allocation narrative:**
- APAC isn't just "executing better" — they have 6× the performance
- APAC's margin expansion to 42% = pricing power (customers paying premiums)
- MEX's tiny backlog = no buffer if constrained market spreads

**Action for Josh:**
> "APAC's performance isn't sustainable if allocation goes global. What's the plan when USA/MEX hit the same supply constraints and can't maintain 42% margins?"
