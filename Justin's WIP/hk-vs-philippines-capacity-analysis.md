## Executive Summary

This analysis compares the cost of expanding material inspection capacity in **Hong Kong** versus establishing operations in the **Philippines** to handle projected 2026 volume growth (3x increase from 2025).

### Key Finding

**Philippines expansion delivers significant cost savings across all scenarios analyzed.**

| Timeframe | Minimum Savings | Maximum Savings |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Monthly | $46,000 | $117,000 |
| Annual | $553,000 | $1,410,000 |
| 5-Year | $2.8M | $7.1M |

## Key Takeaways

1. **Philippines expansion saves $46k - $117k per month** across all scenarios

2. **Annual savings range: $0.6M - $1.4M**

3. **Labor cost differential is the primary driver** (~70% of savings)
   - Material Inspectors: 73% cheaper in PH
   - Senior Inspectors: 71% cheaper in PH
   - Supervisors: 69% cheaper in PH

4. **Facility cost differential adds significant value** (~30% of savings)
   - 89% lower rent in Philippines ($2 vs $18 per sqft/month)

5. **Equipment costs are identical** — expansion location doesn't affect equipment spend

6. **Higher efficiency (1.5x) reduces headcount needs** but savings remain strong because facility savings are unaffected by productivity gains

7. **Even worst-case scenario saves $46k/month** (low volume + high efficiency)
